Key Terms


European Union (EU)

- A political and economic union of 27 European countries committed to shared laws, trade, and regulatory standards.


Brexit

- The 2016 decision and process through which the UK formally exited the EU, finalized in January 2020.


Single Market

- A system that allows the free movement of goods, services, and people across EU countries.


Customs Union

- An agreement between EU member states to charge the same import duties and allow free trade between each other.


Democratic Deficit

- The gap between the performance of democratic institutions and the public’s expectations of those institutions.


Economic Interdependence

- The mutual reliance between countries for the supply of goods and services necessary to their economies.


Sovereignty

- The authority of a state to govern itself without outside interference.


“Take Back Control”

- A popular Brexit slogan emphasizing UK independence and sovereignty from EU regulations.
