Brief History of the UK's Relationship with the EU

1973 – UK Joins the EU (then EEC)

The United Kingdom officially becomes a member of the European Economic Community, which later evolves into the European Union.

2016 – Brexit Referendum

52% of voters choose to leave the EU in a national referendum.

2020 – UK Officially Leaves the EU

The exit process is completed, and the UK formally ends its membership in the European Union.

Post-2020 – Impacts and Public Sentiment

  • Trade disruptions with the EU
  • Decline in foreign investment
  • Labor shortages in key industries
  • Growing interest in rejoining, especially among younger voters
